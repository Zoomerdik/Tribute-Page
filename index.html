Albert Einstein

The Genius Behind the Theory of Relativity

Here is a brief chronology of the life of Albert Einstein:

  • March 14, 1879: Albert Einstein was born in Ulm, Kingdom of WÃ¼rttemberg, Germany.
  • 1896-1900: He studied at the Polytechnic Institute in Zurich, Switzerland.
  • 1905: Published four groundbreaking scientific papers, including the Special Theory of Relativity, the theory of the photoelectric effect, and a paper on the motion of molecules in liquids. This year became known as Einstein's "Annus Mirabilis" or "Miracle Year."
  • 1907: He married Mileva Maric.
  • 1915: Published the General Theory of Relativity, which redefined the concept of gravity.
  • 1919: The British expedition that tested the General Theory of Relativity's prediction of the deflection of starlight by the Sun confirmed the theory, making Einstein a worldwide sensation.
  • 1921: Awarded the Nobel Prize in Physics for his explanation of the photoelectric effect.
  • 1933: Fled Germany due to the Nazi threat and moved to the United States.
  • 1939: Wrote a letter to President Franklin D. Roosevelt warning about the potential development of nuclear weapons and advocating for research in this area.
  • 1945: The United States dropped atomic bombs on Hiroshima and Nagasaki, leading to the end of World War II.

"Albert Einstein was not only a great scientist but also a great man. His impact on our understanding of nature and the cosmos is immeasurable."

-- Niels Bohr, Danish physicist and Nobel laureate
